My pathway into engineering was driven by a general curiosity about technology and how it could be used to solve some of the world’s largest problems. On my journey to becoming an engineer, I realised that I could combine my personal interest in photography and cameras with my enthusiasm for software engineering by pursuing a career in this area.

In 2017, I began studying a Bachelor of Software Engineering at the University of Technology Sydney and have been working in the industry since 2018 as a Full Stack Software Engineer for the Technology 360 Group. In this role, I worked within a Scrum Team to develop human resource management software solutions for clients such as Life Without Barriers, Blooms Chemist and Activ – reducing their payroll costs by up to 10%. Beyond this, I am also collaborating with Blackmagic Design, a renown cinema camera company, on my Honours year capstone project – developing software that can enhance image quality in cameras with small sensors.

Due to my varied industry and academic experience I have developed a strong skill set that allows me to work on advanced systems regardless of programming language, collaborate effectively in any team and take initiative in the workplace.

Research

My Capstone Project

In my honours year at UTS, I have conducted some significant research in order to write a thesis for my Capstone Project. The engineering challenge I selected was to develop a custom debayering algorithm in order to get the highest quality image with the lowest computational cost out of a 10-bit Raspberry Pi Camera. Additionally, the debayering algorithm must be temporally stable so that it can be used to make HDR video without looking noticeably processed.

As a result of my research over the previous semester, I have achieved the following:
- Developing strong foundation knowledge of debayering and its context
- Creating a literature review of over 40 reputable research papers
- Determining which algorithms are best suited to the hardware limitations of the Raspberry Pi
- Setting up the Raspberry Pi for engineering purposes, learning how to extract raw data and cleaning the camera feed
- Setting up quality assessment methods for evaluating performance
- Implementation of various basic demosaicking methods
- Completion of the problem analysis report, project management plan and research proposal

Teamwork & Service

My contributions to various teams

Since the majority of subjects at UTS involve group work, over several years I have gained a lot of experience with working effectively within a team. One specific example of this was Software Studio 3A, where our team of 7 students developed SnakeScanner - shown in the Projects section. For this project, I was the Product Owner meaning I was responsible for delivering my vision of the product, prioritising the work and managing the team. In order to work efficiently as a team, we utilised Trello for task management and Microsoft Teams to conduct bi-weekly meetings. A Visual Studio code plugin called “VS-LiveShare” was also utilised for pair coding amongst team members. In the end, the team achieved a high distinction result, which I believe reflects well on my ability to work well in a team.

Furthermore, I have significant teamwork experience in my position as Software Engineer at the Technology 360 Group. In this role, I work within a real Scrum Team to deliver solutions to large clients. Several meetings are used to ensure open lines of communication are maintained including, daily ‘stand-up’ meetings, sprint planning, refinement meetings, sprint review and sprint retrospective meetings. Additionally, VS-LiveShare and Google Meet are used in conjuction regularly for the purpose of collaborative coding sessions.
